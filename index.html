Ziyang Li is a final year PhD student from the University of Pennsylvania, advised by Prof. Mayur Naik. He works in the intersection of Programming Languages (PL) and Machine Learning (ML), specifically in the area of Neurosymbolic Programming, Program Analysis, and Security. Recently, he is focusing on building and extending the Scallop, a neurosymbolic programming language, that aims to bridge the gap between neural networks and symbolic reasoning components. The language has led to diverse applications in the domain of natural language processing (NLP), computer vision (CV), security, health, and bioinformatics. He is awarded the AWS Fellowship for conducting research on trustworthy AI.

Before his PhD, he obtained his dual Bachelors degree on Computer Science and Mathematics from the UCSD, where he was advised by Prof. Ravi Ramamoorthi on topics within Computer Graphics (CG). He also spent a year working in UCSD Design Lab on HCI with Prof. Scott Klemmer. In his spare time, he is a Piano enthusiast and the keyboardist of the band, The Protagonists, where he plays Jazz, Fusion, and Pop music.
